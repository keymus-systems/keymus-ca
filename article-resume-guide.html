How to Write a Canadian Resume: Format, Tips & Templates

The complete guide to resumes that actually work

Published March 2026 9 min read Keymus Career Team

Your resume is often your first impression with a Canadian employer. Yet many job seekers hurt their chances with resumes that don't follow Canadian conventions. Whether you're a student, career changer, or internationally trained professional, this guide covers everything you need to know about Canadian resume format and content.

Key Insight: Canadian employers typically spend 6–8 seconds scanning each resume. A well-formatted, concise document significantly improves your chances of getting past the initial screen.

The Canadian Resume Format: The Basics

Length

  • Entry-level (0–3 years): 1 page maximum
  • Mid-career (3–10 years): 1–2 pages
  • Senior/Executive (10+ years): 2–3 pages (but 2 is preferred)

Font & Formatting

  • Use professional fonts: Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman (10–12pt)
  • Keep margins between 0.5–1 inch on all sides
  • Use bold and italics sparingly for emphasis
  • Save as PDF to maintain formatting across computers
  • File name: "FirstName_LastName_Resume.pdf" (not "My Resume.pdf")

What Canadian Employers DO Want to See

1. Contact Information

  • Your full name (at the top, larger font)
  • Phone number (with area code)
  • Email address (professional format: firstname.lastname@email.com)
  • City and province (or full address if comfortable)
  • LinkedIn profile URL (optional but recommended)

2. Professional Summary or Objective (Optional)

A 2–3 sentence summary highlighting your value. Use this for career changers or to target specific roles.

Example: "Results-driven software developer with 6 years of experience in full-stack web development. Proven expertise in JavaScript, React, and cloud deployment. Seeking a senior developer role to lead innovative projects in the Toronto tech market."

3. Work Experience

This is the most important section. Format as:

Company Name | Job Title | City, Province | Month Year – Month Year
  • Achievement that impacted the business
  • Responsibility that shows your skills
  • Result with numbers when possible

4. Education

List your highest qualification first:

Degree Name | University Name | Location | Graduation Year
  • Relevant coursework (if recent graduate)
  • Academic honors (Dean's List, scholarships)
  • Minor or specialization

5. Skills Section

List relevant technical and soft skills related to the job posting. Group by category:

  • Technical: Programming languages, software, platforms
  • Languages: English, French, others (specify proficiency level)
  • Certifications: Industry-specific credentials

What Canadian Employers Do NOT Want

DON'T Include:
  • Photo (unless specifically requested)
  • Date of birth
  • Marital/family status
  • Religion or politics
  • Salary expectations
  • Reasons for leaving jobs
  • Personal interests (unless relevant)
  • Your grade point average
DO Include:
  • Keywords from job posting
  • Quantifiable achievements
  • Relevant volunteer work
  • Certifications & licenses
  • Professional affiliations
  • Publications (if relevant)
  • Awards & recognition
  • Links to portfolio/GitHub

ATS Optimization: Getting Past Screening Software

Many Canadian companies use Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) to screen resumes. Follow these rules to ensure your resume is readable by ATS:

  • Use standard headings: "Work Experience," "Education," "Skills" (not creative titles)
  • Include keywords from the job posting: If they say "project management," use that exact term
  • Avoid graphics and tables: Use simple text formatting only
  • Use standard bullet points: Not special characters or symbols
  • Include full company names: Not acronyms (unless the industry standard)
  • Spell out acronyms on first mention: "Automated Applicant Tracking System (ATS)"

Action Verbs That Canadian Employers Love

Start bullet points with strong action verbs:

  • Leadership: Led, managed, directed, oversaw, coordinated
  • Achievement: Achieved, exceeded, delivered, completed, accomplished
  • Improvement: Increased, improved, streamlined, optimized, enhanced
  • Technical: Developed, implemented, deployed, built, engineered
  • Collaboration: Collaborated, partnered, facilitated, supported, assisted

Resume Examples by Industry

Software Developer Resume Highlights

  • Programming languages and frameworks used
  • Projects deployed to production with user metrics
  • Open-source contributions
  • Links to GitHub or portfolio

Healthcare Professional Resume Highlights

  • Certifications (RN, RPN, CNA) and credentials
  • Patient care outcomes improved
  • Shift experience (day, night, weekends)
  • Bilingual skills (especially French/English in Canada)

Skilled Trades Resume Highlights

  • Red Seal certification (recognized nationally)
  • Types of projects completed (residential, commercial, industrial)
  • Equipment and tools expertise
  • Safety certifications (First Aid, WHMIS)

Common Resume Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake #1: Being Too Vague
  • ❌ "Responsible for customer service"
  • ✓ "Resolved customer issues with 95% satisfaction rating; reduced complaints by 30% in 6 months"
Mistake #2: Inconsistent Formatting
  • ❌ Dates in different formats (Jan 2020, 1/2020, January 2020)
  • ✓ Consistent format: "January 2020 – March 2022" throughout
Mistake #3: Including Irrelevant Information
  • ❌ Five years of customer service for a software developer role
  • ✓ Focus on transferable skills and relevant experience

Tailoring Your Resume for Each Application

Don't send the same resume to every employer. Customize:

  • Job title: If applying for "Project Manager," feature project management experience first
  • Keywords: Mirror language from the job posting
  • Summary: Rewrite for each role
  • Bullet point order: Lead with most relevant achievements

Resume Screening: What Gets You to the Next Round?

Recruiters are looking for:

  • ✓ Relevant experience in the field (exact match or strong adjacent)
  • ✓ Required certifications or credentials
  • ✓ Quantifiable results and metrics
  • ✓ Clean formatting with zero errors
  • ✓ Clear demonstration of required skills

Final Checklist Before Submitting

Before hitting "send," verify:
  • ☐ No spelling or grammar errors (use Grammarly or similar)
  • ☐ Consistent formatting throughout
  • ☐ All dates are accurate
  • ☐ Phone number and email are correct
  • ☐ File is named professionally
  • ☐ Saved as PDF (not Word document)
  • ☐ One page for entry-level, 1–2 pages otherwise
  • ☐ Keywords from job posting are included
  • ☐ Action verbs start each bullet point
  • ☐ No personal information (birth date, photo, etc.)
